# 🛡️ HoldLine — User Personas

**ETHGlobal Cannes 2026 · Product Reference Document**

---

## Persona A — "The Anxious Believer" (Primary target)

- **Who:** Crypto holder for 6-24 months, holds between $5K and $50K in crypto
- **Profile:** 25-40 years old, employed, invests part of their savings. Not a trader, not a degen — someone who believes in the technology but isn't an expert
- **Behavior:** Bought after reading articles, watching videos, listening to podcasts. Has a "thesis" in their head but has never written it down. Checks prices 3-5 times a day. Follows Crypto Twitter but gets influenced by opposing opinions
- **Core pain:** When the market drops 20%+, they can't sleep. They oscillate between selling ("what if it keeps dropping?") and holding ("what if it bounces tomorrow?"). No objective decision framework — it's 100% emotional
- **What they say:** *"I had good reasons to buy ETH at $3,000, but when it dropped to $2,200 I couldn't remember them anymore. I almost sold at the worst possible moment."*
- **Entry moment:** After a painful correction where they almost sold (or did sell and regretted it). Or right after buying when they want to "anchor" their conviction

---

## Persona B — "The Disciplined DCA'er" (Secondary target)

- **Who:** Methodical crypto investor, regular DCA ($100-500/month), portfolio $20K-200K
- **Profile:** 30-45 years old, often in tech or finance. Understands blockchain. Has survived 1-2 bear markets already
- **Behavior:** Has a thesis written somewhere (notes, Google Doc, pinned tweet) but never revisits it. Continues DCA mechanically but regularly wonders if the thesis still holds
- **Core pain:** Their thesis is 12-18 months old. The market has changed. New information keeps arriving (regulation, tech, competition). They don't know if their original thesis is still valid or if they're DCA'ing out of inertia
- **What they say:** *"I've been DCA'ing ETH for 18 months. My thesis was L2s + deflationary staking. But with 50 fragmented L2s and the blob fee drama, does my thesis still hold?"*
- **Entry moment:** When they want a thesis "check-up". Or when a major market event shakes their confidence

---

## Persona C — "The Intimidated Crypto-Curious" (Expansion target)

- **Who:** Wants to invest in crypto but paralyzed by complexity and fear of making mistakes
- **Profile:** 25-50 years old, has savings, hears about crypto from friends/media. Has never bought or put in a small amount on an exchange without real conviction
- **Behavior:** Reads articles, watches videos but never takes action because they lack a framework. Doesn't know where to start. Afraid of "entering at the wrong time"
- **Core pain:** They don't have a thesis — they don't even know how to build one. Every piece of content says "DYOR" but nobody explains how to structure their thinking
- **What they say:** *"I want to invest in crypto but I don't know where to start. Everyone says it's a good time but I don't understand why."*
- **Entry moment:** Friend recommendation, or ad after a bull run. HoldLine helps them build their first thesis before they even buy
